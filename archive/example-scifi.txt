Chapter One: The Signal

The colony ship Odyssey had been drifting through the void for three hundred years when the signal arrived. Commander Sarah Chen was on her routine inspection of the cryogenic bay when the alarms began to wail.

"All hands to stations," the ship's AI announced, its synthetic voice echoing through the empty corridors. "Unknown transmission detected. Origin: Unknown. Distance: Forty-two light years."

Sarah's heart raced as she sprinted toward the bridge. In three centuries of silent travel, they had detected nothing. No signs of intelligent life. No hints that humanity wasn't alone in the universe. Until now.

The bridge was already occupied when she arrived. Lieutenant Marcus Webb sat at the communications console, his fingers dancing across holographic displays. His face was pale.

"What do we have?" Sarah demanded.

Marcus looked up, and she saw something in his eyes she'd never seen before. Fear.

"Commander, you need to hear this," he said quietly. "It's repeating. Over and over." He pressed a button.

The sound that filled the bridge was unlike anything Sarah had ever heard. It wasn't words, exactly, but something deeper. A pattern. A rhythm. And underneath it all, something that sounded almost like... laughter.

"How long until we reach the source?" Sarah asked.

"At current velocity," the AI interjected, "seventeen days, four hours, and twenty-three minutes."

Sarah nodded slowly. Seventeen days to find out if humanity's first contact with alien intelligence would be the greatest discovery in history, or its final mistake.

The signal continued to pulse through the darkness, patient and insistent, as the Odyssey adjusted its course toward whatever waited in the black.
